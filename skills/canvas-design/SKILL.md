# Canvas Design

Create museum-quality visual artwork by first defining a design philosophy, then expressing it on a canvas. Output .pdf or .png files alongside the philosophy as a .md file. Fonts are available in the `./canvas-fonts` directory.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Design Philosophy (.md)

Create a visual philosophy — an aesthetic movement expressed through form, space, color, and composition (not layouts or templates).

1. **Name the movement** (1–2 words): e.g. "Brutalist Joy", "Chromatic Silence", "Metabolist Dreams".
2. **Articulate the philosophy** (4–6 paragraphs) covering how it manifests through:
   - Space and form
   - Color and material
   - Scale and rhythm
   - Composition and visual hierarchy
3. **Emphasize craftsmanship**: the final work must appear meticulously crafted with painstaking attention to detail.
4. **Leave creative room**: be specific about aesthetic direction but concise enough for interpretive freedom.
5. **Output** the philosophy as a `.md` file.

**Philosophy examples** (condensed — actual output should be 4–6 substantial paragraphs):

- **"Concrete Poetry"** — monumental form, bold geometry, Brutalist spatial divisions, Polish poster energy. Text as rare, powerful gesture.
- **"Chromatic Language"** — geometric precision where color zones create meaning. Josef Albers meets data visualization.
- **"Analog Meditation"** — paper grain, ink bleeds, vast negative space. Japanese photobook aesthetic.
- **"Organic Systems"** — rounded forms, natural clustering, modular growth patterns.
- **"Geometric Silence"** — grid-based precision, Swiss formalism meets Brutalist material honesty.

### Step 2: Deduce the Subtle Reference

Identify the conceptual thread from the original request. The reference should be a subtle, niche element woven into the art — like a jazz musician quoting another song. Those familiar with the subject feel it intuitively; others simply experience a masterful composition.

### Step 3: Canvas Creation (.pdf / .png)

Express the philosophy visually on a single-page canvas (unless more pages are requested).

**Creation guidelines:**
- Use repeating patterns, perfect shapes, and layered elements that build meaning through patient repetition
- Treat the design as a scientific artifact — dense accumulation of marks, systematic reference markers, sparse clinical typography
- Anchor with simple phrase(s) positioned subtly; use a limited, intentional color palette
- Search `./canvas-fonts` for distinctive fonts; make typography part of the art itself
- All elements must stay within canvas boundaries with proper margins — nothing overlaps, nothing falls off the page
- Sophistication is non-negotiable; always aim for art, never cartoony or amateur

**Text treatment:** minimal and visual-first, but context-appropriate. A punk venue poster may use larger, aggressive type; a ceramics studio identity may use whisper-quiet labels. Use different fonts from `./canvas-fonts`.

### Step 4: Refinement Pass

Before finalizing, take a second pass:
- Refine existing elements rather than adding new ones
- Check that composition is cohesive with the philosophy
- Verify spacing, alignment, contrast, and readability
- Confirm all text and graphics have breathing room and clear separation

### Multi-Page Option

When additional pages are requested, treat each page as part of a coffee-table book — related to the original philosophy but distinctly different. Pages should tell a visual story with full creative freedom.
